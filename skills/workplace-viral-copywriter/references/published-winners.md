## 2026-05-15: Salary Does Not Deserve My Mood

Observed performance:
- About 16,772 reads
- 411 likes
- 932 shares
- 202 favorites/likes
- 61 comments

Winning factors:
- Broad emotion: low salary should not consume expensive mood.
- Safe to share: self-comforting, not attacking a named person.
- Simple structure: short image-ready paired lines, often setup plus relief.
- Clear identity: low-energy capybara as emotional stop-loss mascot.
- Low aggression: the piece feels like "advising myself to calm down", not yelling at work.
- Sequential comfort: the images build a small emotional path rather than each line trying to be a headline.

Useful structure:
- Work is livelihood, not life.
- Do not get too invested.
- Coworkers are work partners.
- No forced relationships.
- The point is making money.
- Salary is not worth damaging your mood.
- Do not let work consume the most precious self.

Recommended adaptation:
- Generate 5-6 paired groups.
- In each group, the upper line names one workplace scene.
- The lower line helps the reader put the emotion down.
- Keep at least half the lines gentle and ordinary, so the stronger lines feel more credible.

## Use In Future Posts

Prioritize these families:
- salary does not deserve emotional cost
- absurd comparison between work and begging
- coworkers as low-cost work relationships
- emotional stop-loss and self-protection

Do not overfit to one phrase. Rotate the complaint target and surface wording.
Do not overfit to "sentence as slogan". The winning rhythm is closer to paired self-comfort: scene first, then emotional release.
