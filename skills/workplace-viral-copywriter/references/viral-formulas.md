# Viral Story Formulas

Use these formulas to generate original story-first copy. They are patterns, not fixed lines.

## Article Skeleton

Default article length: 10-14 image-ready lines, organized as 5-7 paired image groups.

The article should feel like one readable miniature story:

1. `起因`: The character has one harmless plan.
2. `误入`: A scene detail creates temptation or friction.
3. `加码`: Several details appear individually reasonable.
4. `失控`: The basket, time, emotion, or promise gets bigger.
5. `账单`: Money, time, effort, or embarrassment lands.
6. `反应`: The character goes silent, suspects something, comforts themselves, or lightly surrenders.
7. `收束`: One line reframes the whole story.

Use this format:

- `第1组 上：...`
- `第1组 下：...`

Each pair should be related:
- Upper line: scene, action, object, or new detail.
- Lower line: live reaction, contradiction, escalation, or punchline.

## Strong Story Shapes

### 小额破财 / 超市做局

Core emotion: self-exposed spending contradiction.

Story chain:
- I only came in for one thing.
- A cheap item looks too reasonable.
- A discount makes not buying feel like losing money.
- Everything looks harmless alone.
- The basket becomes suspiciously full.
- The checkout number exposes the whole illusion.
- Ending: I was not shopping; I was slowly persuaded.

Useful objects:
- shopping basket, price tag, 9.9 sign, second-item-half-price label, receipt, checkout screen, wallet, plastic bag.

Example patterns:
- `我只是想买一瓶水`
- `购物篮不知道什么时候到了我手上`
- `每样都不贵，放一起就不对了`
- `小票出来那一刻，我突然很尊重数学`

### 熬夜拖延 / 睡前失控

Core emotion: wants rest but keeps delaying rest.

Story chain:
- I promise to sleep early.
- I only check one message/video/item.
- The phone gives another small reason to stay.
- Time quietly becomes late.
- I start bargaining with tomorrow.
- Ending: I am not staying up; I am negotiating with sleep.

Useful objects:
- phone, bed, slippers, lamp, clock, charger, blanket, eye mask.

### 外卖选择 / 省钱失败

Core emotion: wants cheap, pays through coupons and add-ons.

Story chain:
- I open the app to order something simple.
- Delivery fee, starting price, discount, and full reduction appear.
- I add one more item to make the coupon work.
- The discount looks large but the total grows.
- Ending: I did not save money; I helped the coupon complete its dream.

Useful objects:
- phone, delivery app, coupon, meal box, chopsticks, receipt.

### 出门反悔 / 社交电量不足

Core emotion: prepared outside, reversed inside.

Story chain:
- I already changed clothes and reached the door.
- Shoes, bag, and keys are ready.
- A tiny thought says maybe next time.
- The character sits back down.
- Ending: I did not cancel the plan; my energy cancelled me.

Useful objects:
- doorway, shoes, bag, keys, mirror, sofa, umbrella.

### 职场低能量 / 情绪止损

Core emotion: work can happen, but the self should not be overpaid into it.

Story chain:
- A small work message or meeting appears.
- The body reacts faster than the brain.
- The character almost gives too much emotion.
- One ordinary detail reminds them to slow down.
- Ending: work can wait a little; I should return myself to myself.

Useful objects:
- phone popup, work badge, meeting room, elevator, coffee, calendar, laptop.

## Title Formulas

- `我本来只是想___`
- `有一种破产，叫___`
- `___的时候，人会变得很离谱`
- `每样都不贵，___就不对了`
- `我不是___，我是___`
- `本来只想___，最后___`
- `我怀疑___给我做局`
- `成年人最容易输给___`
- `这件小事，怎么越想越不对`
- `我以为我很克制，直到___`

## Weak To Strong

Weak: `我买太多东西了`
Strong: `我只是想买瓶水，购物篮自己长到我手上`
More story-first: `进门前我还很清醒，看到9块9以后就开始不像我了`

Weak: `东西都不贵`
Strong: `每样看着都不贵，结账跳到100多`
More story-first: `我一路都在捡便宜，小票出来才发现便宜在捡我`

Weak: `我熬夜了`
Strong: `我说看一眼手机，结果天快亮了`
More story-first: `我只是想确认一下闹钟，结果手机把我留下来加班`

Weak: `我不想出门`
Strong: `都走到门口了，我又把自己劝回去了`
More story-first: `钥匙都拿在手上了，我突然觉得沙发也挺需要我`

Weak: `下班别回消息`
Strong: `下班后慢一点回，也是在把自己还给自己`
More story-first: `手机亮了一下，我差点又把下班交出去`

## Grounded Language Rules

Prefer:
- `我一下就不说话了`
- `怎么越看越不对`
- `我感觉自己被做局了`
- `这几块钱单看都挺老实`
- `小票出来以后我懂了`
- `我不是想买，是它太会摆了`

Avoid:
- `在消费主义的洪流里重新审视自我`
- `生活以荒诞的方式完成了对我的教育`
- `成年人终将在欲望与秩序之间和解`
- `这是一场关于克制与失控的隐喻`
- `我与命运达成了某种默契`

If a sentence sounds elevated, translate it back into a thing a tired person would actually say at the checkout counter, doorway, bed, elevator, or desk.

## Final Checks

Before returning copy, check:
- Can the whole piece be retold as one story?
- Does each pair add a new detail rather than repeat the same point?
- Are at least five lines visual enough to draw directly?
- Is the ending a natural payoff, not a lecture?
- Does the language sound like real everyday speech rather than high-end copywriting?
- Did the copy avoid copying any reference image wording beyond generic everyday phrases?
